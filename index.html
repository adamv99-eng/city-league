Foundation & Fun-First
Players begin their City Soccer journey at age 5. City Soccer Prep builds basic skills in a structured, fun-first environment designed to instill love for the game and introduce players to the City Soccer identity.
Structured Youth Progression
Age-grouped tracks provide structured coaching, tactical education, and competitive play matched to each stage — preparing every player for competitive league entry.
Assessment & Placement
Open Academy bridges youth programming to competitive league play. Players are assessed and placed into the appropriate City League tier — ensuring every athlete competes at the right level from day one.
Broad Community Access
Division 2 opens the City League to the full cross-section of New Orleans soccer — local clubs, community orgs, and developing players building toward elite competition.
The Competitive Core
Division 1 is where elite talent sharpens. U20/U23 rosters trained by assigned coaches, mentored by experienced adults, with game film review at The City Hub. The primary pipeline to Regional Premier.
The Destination
City League-operated under the UPSL framework. Top U20/U23 performers compete at the highest regional level — earning scouting visibility, Play-In positioning, and real professional pathways.

New Orleans Context
Built on realistic local conditions. The New Orleans Jesters (NPSL) serve as a comparable benchmark for Premier-level play — a concrete reference point for what competing at this level actually requires.
Sufficient metro talent exists to field four genuine Premier-level teams without a full travel-team model.
Travel costs are prohibitive for elite adults given regional geography — local-first is the sustainable path.
U20/U23-driven rosters make Premier play financially viable while generating family investment.
Elite adults exceeding local levels are guided toward full professional opportunities — keeping the Incubator focused on player growth.
Thorough registration and vetting minimize mid-season dropout and protect competitive integrity at every tier.
INFRASTRUCTURE
Infrastructure
🏟️
Game Day Stadium
Official UPSL match days hosted at an established venue such as Pan American Stadium — delivering a professional experience for players, families, and the broader New Orleans soccer community. Reinforces legitimacy and gives Incubator players the authentic competitive atmosphere their growth demands.
⚽
Soccer Specific Facility
A purpose-built turf practice field (min. 100×65 yards) with technical training areas forms the daily operational backbone. Secured through a 10+ year metro-area lease, enabling the consistent high-frequency training environment that genuine player growth demands.
🎯
The City Hub
Centralized at the practice facility, The City Hub is the cultural and operational core of City Soccer. It hosts game film review, strategic sessions, social events, and community programming — building cohesion off the field and reinforcing this is more than a league. It's a player growth community.
Regional Expansion Blueprint
The City Soccer model is deliberately architected for replication. Two to three comparable central sites — each anchored by their own hub facility — can replicate the full player growth ecosystem, with New Orleans as the proven blueprint.
🗺️
City · State · Regional Scaling

Defined expansion criteria at each level ensure new markets join with appropriate infrastructure, competitive readiness, and community ties in place — not simply as added teams filling a schedule.

🤝
Shared Programming Model

The community programming, vetting, and registration framework built in New Orleans is shared with expansion partners — maintaining consistency in quality and identity across all sites.

📅
Year-Round Programming Calendar

Challenge Cup tournaments, visiting summer teams, College Prep events, and Regional Premier club exposure sessions create a 12-month program — a full growth environment, not just a season.
